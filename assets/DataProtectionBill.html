The Data Protection Bill, 2024

The Data Protection Bill, 2024 is a comprehensive legislative framework designed to safeguard the privacy and security of personal data in Kenya. This bill establishes guidelines for:

  • The collection, processing, and storage of personal information.
  • The rights and responsibilities of data subjects and data controllers.

1. Introduction

The Data Protection Bill, 2024 addresses the need for a robust legal framework to protect individuals' personal data. It aims to enhance transparency and accountability in the handling of personal information by setting clear guidelines and standards for data protection.

2. Key Provisions

The bill outlines several key provisions, including:

  • Requirements for data controllers regarding data collection and processing.
  • Rights of data subjects to access, correct, and delete their personal data.
  • Obligations for organizations to implement data protection measures and report breaches.
  • Establishment of a regulatory body to oversee compliance and address grievances.

3. Responsibilities of Data Controllers

Data controllers are required to:

  • Ensure that personal data is collected and processed lawfully and transparently.
  • Implement appropriate security measures to protect data from unauthorized access or loss.
  • Provide clear information to data subjects about their rights and how their data is used.
  • Notify the regulatory body and affected individuals in the event of a data breach.

4. Rights of Data Subjects

Data subjects have the following rights under the bill:

  • The right to access their personal data and obtain copies.
  • The right to request correction or deletion of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • The right to object to the processing of their data for certain purposes.
  • The right to lodge a complaint with the regulatory body if their rights are violated.

5. Compliance and Enforcement

The bill provides for:

  • Regular audits and inspections to ensure compliance with data protection standards.
  • Penalties for non-compliance, including fines and sanctions.
  • Mechanisms for data subjects to seek redress and compensation for violations of their data protection rights.

6. Conclusion

The Data Protection Bill, 2024 represents a significant step towards ensuring that personal data is handled with the highest standards of privacy and security. By setting out clear responsibilities and rights, the bill aims to build trust and confidence in how personal data is managed in Kenya.
