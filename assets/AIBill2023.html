Kenya Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Society Bill, 2023

The Kenya Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Society Bill, 2023 seeks to establish a robust framework for the regulation and development of robotics and artificial intelligence (AI) technologies in Kenya. This bill proposes the formation of the Kenya Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Society to oversee and guide the growth of these innovative sectors.

1. Introduction

The bill aims to promote a conducive environment for the growth of robotics and AI technologies by setting up necessary regulatory frameworks and ethical guidelines. It encourages public and private sectors to collaborate in advancing AI and robotics to address societal challenges and drive economic growth.

2. Objectives of the Bill

  • To create a regulatory framework that promotes innovation in robotics and AI while safeguarding public interest and security.
  • To establish ethical standards for the development and deployment of AI technologies.
  • To foster collaboration between government bodies, private sector entities, and academic institutions.
  • To position Kenya as a leader in technological innovation on the African continent and globally.

3. Establishment of the Kenya Robotics and AI Society

The bill mandates the creation of the Kenya Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Society (KRAIS), which will be the central body responsible for overseeing all activities related to robotics and AI in the country. The society will consist of representatives from various sectors, including government ministries, academic and research institutions, private sector companies involved in AI and robotics, and NGOs and civil society groups.

4. Responsibilities of KRAIS

The KRAIS will be responsible for:

  • Setting ethical guidelines for AI development and deployment.
  • Ensuring compliance with national and international laws and standards.
  • Facilitating research and development in AI and robotics.
  • Providing a platform for stakeholder engagement and public consultation.
  • Promoting education and awareness about robotics and AI among the Kenyan populace.

5. Roles of National and County Governments

Under this bill, the roles of the national and county governments include:

  • Formulating policies to support innovation in robotics and AI.
  • Providing funding and infrastructure support for AI research and development.
  • Ensuring the integration of AI and robotics into various sectors such as healthcare, education, agriculture, and manufacturing.
  • Collaborating with KRAIS to enforce ethical standards and regulations.

6. Penalties for Non-Compliance

The bill stipulates stringent penalties for non-compliance with the established regulations. These include:

  • Fines ranging from KES 500,000 to KES 5,000,000 depending on the severity of the violation.
  • Imprisonment for up to 5 years for deliberate non-compliance or gross negligence.
  • Revocation of licenses for companies or entities found in breach of the ethical guidelines or safety standards.

7. Conclusion

This bill aims to create a balanced environment where innovation in robotics and AI can thrive, contributing to Kenya's socio-economic development while ensuring the protection of public interests and ethical standards. By fostering collaboration across sectors and emphasizing ethical AI, the Kenya Robotics and AI Bill, 2023, is a step towards a technologically advanced and responsible future for Kenya.
